Our Mission

ClaimCheck helps everyday people find class action settlements they qualify for, understand how insurance claims work, and navigate the process of getting money they are legally owed.

Most people leave thousands of dollars on the table every year simply because they do not know a settlement exists or do not know how to file. Class action lawsuits against major corporations are settled every week. Millions of eligible claimants never collect because the process feels complicated or they never heard about it.

We change that. ClaimCheck publishes clear, plain-language guides on open settlements, how to check your eligibility, how to file a claim, and what to do when an insurance company denies or underpays your claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ClaimCheck free?

Yes. All guides and information on ClaimCheck are completely free. We never charge for settlement information. We make money through display advertising, not from you.

How do I find settlements I qualify for?

Start on our home page, which lists major open settlements. You can also browse our blog for recent class action news, or search for settlements by company name or product type.

What should I do if my insurance claim is denied?

Do not accept the first denial as final. You have the right to appeal. Our guides on appealing denied insurance claims walk you through the process step by step, including what documentation to gather and how to escalate to your state insurance commissioner.

How long does a class action settlement take to pay out?

It varies. Simple settlements can pay within a few months of the claims deadline. Complex ones with many claimants and appeals can take one to three years. Our guides explain the typical timeline and what delays to expect.
