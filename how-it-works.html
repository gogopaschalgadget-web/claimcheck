How ClaimCheck Works

Find Settlements

Our database searches thousands of open class action lawsuit settlements from legitimate sources. We verify each settlement's legitimacy and deadline.

Check Eligibility

Each settlement has specific eligibility criteria. Use our eligibility checker to quickly determine if you qualify. If you're unsure, the settlement website's FAQ section can provide clarification.

Gather Documents

Different settlements require different documentation. We provide a checklist of documents you'll need to file your claim successfully. Most settlements only require proof of purchase and identification.

File Your Claim

Follow the step-by-step filing instructions on each settlement page. Most claims can be filed online directly through the settlement administrator's website. The process typically takes 15-30 minutes.

Track Your Claim

After filing, keep your confirmation number. You can track your claim status on the settlement website. Most claims are processed within 30-90 days.

Get Paid

Once your claim is approved, you'll receive payment via check or ACH transfer. Settlement payments typically arrive 30-180 days after approval.

FAQs

Is this a scam?

No. Class action settlements are legitimate legal proceedings. Top settlement sites like TopClassActions.com and ClassAction.org have been operating for 15+ years. We only list settlements from verified sources.

Do I need to pay to file a claim?

No. Filing claims is completely free. Be suspicious of any website asking for payment to file. Settlement administration is funded by the settling companies, not claimants.

How much will I get?

It depends on the settlement size and number of claims filed. Our estimates are based on available settlement information, but actual amounts may vary. Larger settlements with fewer claims typically result in higher individual payouts.

What if my claim is denied?

If your claim is denied, you can usually appeal through the settlement administrator's process. Read the settlement agreement carefully for appeal procedures.

Can I file multiple claims?

Generally, no. Settlement terms specify that each person can file only one claim per settlement. Attempting to file multiple claims is fraud.
