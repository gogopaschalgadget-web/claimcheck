Data Breach Settlements Still Open in 2026

Data breaches have become the single largest source of class action settlements in the United States. If a company stored your personal information and it was compromised in a breach, you likely qualify for compensation — even if you never experienced identity theft as a result. Courts have increasingly recognized that the mere exposure of personal data causes harm, which means the bar for eligibility is lower than most people realize.

T-Mobile Data Breach Settlement

Deadline: Check website for updates Up to $25,000+ per claimant

T-Mobile's massive data breach affected tens of millions of current and former customers. If you were a T-Mobile customer, your name, Social Security number, driver's license information, and other personal data may have been exposed. The settlement fund provides cash payments, identity theft protection services, and reimbursement for documented losses. Even if you've already received a preliminary notice, you may need to take additional steps to maximize your payout.

Who qualifies: Current and former T-Mobile customers, including T-Mobile prepaid, Sprint, and Metro by T-Mobile users whose data was compromised.

Healthcare Data Breach Settlements

Deadline: Various — check each case $100–$5,000+ per claimant

Multiple healthcare organizations have settled class action lawsuits over data breaches exposing patient records. These settlements typically cover patients whose medical records, insurance information, or Social Security numbers were exposed. Healthcare data is particularly valuable to identity thieves, which is why courts tend to approve larger per-person payouts in these cases.

Who qualifies: Patients of affected healthcare systems who received breach notification letters or whose records were accessed during the breach period.

Data breach settlements are among the easiest to claim because companies are required to notify affected individuals. If you've received any email, letter, or text about a data breach in the past three years, search your records — that notification likely included a claim filing link. Don't assume the deadline has passed. Many settlements extend their filing windows, and new settlements are approved regularly.

Pro tip: Check your email for messages containing words like "settlement," "class action," "data breach," or "your rights." Many people delete these thinking they're spam, but they're legitimate notifications about money you're owed. Learn more about how class action settlements work.

Consumer Product Settlements

Consumer product class action lawsuits in 2026 cover everything from defective appliances to misleading product labels. These settlements are filed when companies sell products that don't work as advertised, contain undisclosed ingredients, or pose safety risks. The good news is that many of these settlements don't require proof of purchase — if you can reasonably demonstrate you bought the product, you can file a claim.

Automotive Defect Settlements

Deadline: Various active cases $500–$10,000+ per vehicle

Several major automakers are facing class action lawsuits over defective transmissions, faulty emissions systems, and premature engine failures. These cases typically cover specific model years and vehicle lines. If you've experienced unexpected mechanical failures, excessive oil consumption, or transmission problems with your vehicle, there may be an active settlement covering your exact situation. Compensation can include cash payments, extended warranty coverage, or buyback offers.

Who qualifies: Owners and lessees of affected vehicle models within the specified model year ranges.

Food & Beverage Mislabeling Settlements

Deadline: Rolling — new cases monthly $5–$50 per product (no receipt needed)

Courts continue to approve settlements against food and beverage companies for misleading labels. Common claims include products marketed as "natural" that contain artificial ingredients, misleading serving sizes, inaccurate calorie counts, and deceptive "made in USA" claims. While individual payouts tend to be smaller, these cases rarely require proof of purchase — you just need to attest that you bought the product.

Who qualifies: Anyone who purchased the specific product during the class period, typically within the last 2-5 years.

Financial & Banking Settlements

Financial institutions are frequent targets of class action lawsuits, and for good reason. From hidden overdraft fees to mortgage servicing errors, banks and lenders have paid out billions in settlements over practices that affected millions of ordinary customers. If you've had a bank account, credit card, mortgage, or student loan in the past five years, it's worth checking whether any open class action lawsuits in 2026 apply to your financial accounts.

Overdraft Fee & Junk Fee Settlements

Deadline: Multiple active cases $50–$500+ per account holder

Multiple banks and credit unions are settling lawsuits over deceptive overdraft practices. These cases typically involve banks charging overdraft fees on transactions that didn't actually overdraw the account, reordering transactions to maximize fees, or charging fees on transactions the customer had opted out of overdraft protection for. If you've ever been hit with what felt like unfair overdraft fees, there may be a settlement with your name on it.

Who qualifies: Account holders at the specific bank who were charged overdraft or insufficient fund fees during the class period.

Student Loan Servicing Settlements

Deadline: Check individual cases $100–$2,000+ per borrower

Student loan servicers have faced class actions for misapplying payments, providing incorrect information about repayment plans, failing to properly process income-driven repayment applications, and charging inappropriate late fees. These settlements can provide direct cash payments and, in some cases, correction of your loan balance or credit report. If you've had issues with your student loan servicer, search for active settlements against that company.

Who qualifies: Borrowers who had loans serviced by the specific company during the class period and experienced the described issues.

Banking settlements often have large class sizes, which can dilute individual payouts. However, they also tend to have high claim rates because banks maintain records that make it easy to verify eligibility. The key is to file as early as possible and provide documentation of your losses if you want to receive more than the base payment amount. Technology & Social Media Settlements

Tech companies collect enormous amounts of personal data, and when they misuse it or fail to protect it, the resulting class action settlements can be substantial. From biometric data violations to anti-competitive practices, technology sector settlements have produced some of the largest per-person payouts in class action history. Here are the major categories of tech settlements open in 2026.

Biometric Data Privacy Settlements

Deadline: Various active cases $100–$400+ per person

States with biometric privacy laws (like Illinois' BIPA) have generated a wave of class action settlements against companies that collected fingerprints, facial scans, or voiceprints without proper consent. These settlements cover everything from employee time clocks that used fingerprint scanners to photo apps that used facial recognition without disclosure. If you've lived or worked in a state with biometric privacy protections, you may be eligible for multiple settlements simultaneously.

Who qualifies: Residents of states with biometric privacy laws (primarily Illinois, but expanding) whose biometric data was collected by the defendant company.

Privacy Violation & Tracking Settlements

Deadline: Multiple cases open $50–$5,000+ per person

Several major tech and social media companies are facing settlements over unauthorized tracking, data sharing with third parties, and violations of wiretapping laws. These cases have expanded significantly as courts recognize that tracking users' online activity without proper disclosure violates state and federal privacy laws. Some settlements cover specific app users while others cover anyone who visited certain websites.

Who qualifies: Users of the specific platform, app, or website during the class period who were subject to the tracking or data collection practices.

Watch out for scams: Legitimate class action settlement administrators will never ask you to pay a fee to file your claim. If someone contacts you asking for money to process your settlement, it's a scam. Always verify settlement details through official court records or trusted sources like ClaimCheck.

Healthcare & Pharmaceutical Settlements

Healthcare class action lawsuits in 2026 cover a range of issues from pharmaceutical side effects to medical device failures and insurance coverage disputes. These cases often involve serious injuries and correspondingly larger settlement amounts. Even if you haven't experienced severe side effects, you may qualify for compensation if you purchased a recalled drug or device.

Pharmaceutical Product Settlements

Deadline: Check specific cases $500–$50,000+ depending on documented harm

Multiple pharmaceutical companies are settling lawsuits over medications with undisclosed side effects, contaminated products, and misleading marketing. These settlements typically create tiered payment structures where people who experienced documented medical issues receive higher payments, while those who purchased but didn't experience side effects receive smaller base amounts. If you've taken a medication that was later recalled or subject to new safety warnings, check if there's an open settlement.

Who qualifies: Anyone who purchased or was prescribed the specific medication during the class period. Higher compensation for those with documented medical issues.

Insurance Coverage Denial Settlements

Deadline: Various Reimbursement of denied claims + cash payments

Health insurance companies face class actions over systematic denial of coverage for specific treatments, improper application of prior authorization requirements, and failure to cover emergency room visits. These settlements can result in reimbursement of out-of-pocket costs you paid for denied claims, plus additional cash payments. If you've had a claim denied by your health insurer that you believed should have been covered, search for active lawsuits against your specific insurer.

Who qualifies: Policyholders whose claims were denied under the specific practices challenged in the lawsuit.

How to File Your Claim: Step by Step

Filing a class action claim is simpler than most people think. The process is designed to be accessible without hiring a lawyer, and most claims can be completed online in under 15 minutes. Here's exactly what to do when you find a settlement you're eligible for.

Step 1: Verify your eligibility. Read the settlement notice carefully. It will specify who qualifies based on factors like the date of purchase, geographic location, or specific product model. If you're unsure whether you qualify, file anyway — the worst that can happen is your claim gets rejected.

Step 2: Gather your documentation. For most settlements, you'll need basic personal information (name, address, email). Some require proof of purchase like receipts, bank statements, or order confirmation emails. Many settlements allow you to file without receipts for a reduced base payment. Search your email for order confirmations or check your bank statements for relevant transactions.

Step 3: Complete the claim form. Visit the official settlement website listed in the notice. Fill out the online claim form with your information. Be accurate — false claims are a federal offense. Most forms take 5-10 minutes to complete.

Step 4: Submit and save your confirmation. After submitting, save your confirmation number and any email receipts. Settlement payouts can take 6-18 months depending on the case, so you'll want this information for your records.

Step 5: Watch for follow-up requests. Some settlement administrators may contact you for additional information or documentation. Respond promptly to avoid having your claim dismissed.

Important: Never pay to file a class action claim. The entire process is free. Attorney fees are paid from the settlement fund, not by individual claimants. If someone asks you for money, it's not a legitimate settlement. For more detail, read our complete guide on how class action settlements work.

Frequently Asked Questions About Open Class Action Lawsuits

Do I need a lawyer to file a class action claim?

No. The lawyers who filed the lawsuit represent the entire class. You simply need to fill out the claim form. There's no cost to you, and you don't need to hire your own attorney. The class attorneys are paid from the settlement fund.

How long does it take to get paid?

Most settlements take 6-18 months from the claim deadline to distribute payments. Some cases take longer if there are appeals or a large number of claims to process. Payment typically arrives as a check or direct deposit, depending on what you selected when filing.

Can I file a claim if I don't have a receipt?

In many cases, yes. Many settlements offer a "no proof of purchase" option with a smaller base payment. You attest under oath that you purchased the product, and you receive a reduced payout. If you do have receipts or bank statements, submitting them typically qualifies you for a higher payment tier.

Is settlement money taxable?

It depends on the type of settlement. Compensation for physical injuries is generally not taxable. Payments for lost wages, emotional distress, or punitive damages typically are taxable. Cash payments from consumer product settlements are usually considered taxable income. Consult a tax professional if you receive a large settlement payment.

What happens if I miss the deadline?

Unfortunately, if the claim deadline has passed, you generally cannot file. This is why it's crucial to act quickly when you learn about a settlement. Some cases do reopen or extend deadlines, but you shouldn't count on this. The best strategy is to check for new settlements regularly and file as soon as you find one you qualify for.
