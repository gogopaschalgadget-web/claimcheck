The 5 Biggest Class Action Settlements of 2026 (So Far)

March 23, 2026 · News · 4 min read

2026 has already produced several massive settlements affecting millions of consumers. From data breaches to defective products, here are the biggest ones — and whether you might qualify for a payout.

1. Major Healthcare Data Breach Settlement

$350 Million

One of the largest healthcare data breaches in history exposed the personal and medical information of over 30 million patients. The settlement fund covers identity theft monitoring, out-of-pocket losses up to $5,000, and cash payments for time spent dealing with the breach.

Who Qualifies: Patients notified of the breach
Est. Payout: $50-$500+ per person
Deadline: Claims Still Open

2. Smartphone Throttling Class Action

$175 Million

A major smartphone manufacturer agreed to settle claims that it intentionally slowed older phone models through software updates without informing consumers. Anyone who owned an affected model during the specified period may qualify.

Who Qualifies: Owners of affected models (2021-2024)
Est. Payout: $35-$85 per device
Deadline: Filing Closes Soon

3. Banking Overdraft Fee Settlement

$120 Million

A large national bank settled claims that it reordered customer transactions to maximize overdraft fees — processing the largest transactions first to drain accounts faster, triggering multiple smaller overdraft charges that wouldn't have occurred otherwise.

Who Qualifies: Customers charged overdraft fees (2019-2025)
Est. Payout: $50-$200 per customer
Deadline: Claims Still Open

4. Auto Defect Recall Settlement

$95 Million

An automaker settled claims related to a defective transmission system that caused unexpected acceleration and difficulty stopping. The settlement covers repair costs, diminished vehicle value, and cash payments for affected owners.

Who Qualifies: Owners of specified models (2020-2023)
Est. Payout: $200-$3,000 depending on repairs
Deadline: Claims Still Open

5. Social Media Privacy Settlement

$65 Million

A social media platform settled claims that it collected biometric data (facial recognition) from users without proper consent, violating state privacy laws. Affected users in certain states can file for cash payments.

Who Qualifies: Users in affected states who uploaded photos
Est. Payout: $30-$150 per person
Deadline: Filing Closes Soon
Filing Tip: For all of these settlements, filing takes 5-10 minutes. Even if the estimated payout seems small, the actual payment often ends up higher because so few people file. There's no downside to submitting a claim — it costs nothing and takes minutes.
