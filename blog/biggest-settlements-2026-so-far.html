The Biggest Class Action Settlements of 2026: Complete Guide

March 23, 2026 · Updated March 26, 2026 · News · 12 min read

2026 is shaping up to be a record year for class action settlements. From massive data breaches to deceptive business practices, companies are paying billions to settle claims with consumers. The catch: most people never file.

If you've received a data breach notification, owned a defective vehicle, or been charged unfair fees, you could be sitting on unclaimed money. This guide covers the 9 biggest settlements of 2026, who qualifies, what to expect, and how to file before deadlines close.

The 9 Biggest Settlements of 2026

1. Capital One Data Breach Settlement

$425 Million

One of the largest bank data breaches in U.S. history exposed the personal and financial information of over 100 million customers. The breach included Social Security numbers, credit card numbers, routing numbers, and bank account numbers, affecting both current and former customers of Capital One.

Who Qualifies

  • Capital One customers notified of the breach by mail or email
  • Customers whose information was exposed between January 2005 and July 2019
  • Anyone who was notified and received the settlement notice

What You Get

  • Cash payments ranging from $50 to $5,000 depending on damages and documentation
  • Free credit monitoring for 3 years
  • Identity theft protection services
  • Reimbursement for costs related to the breach (up to $1,500)
Est. Payout: $50-$500+ per person
Deadline: Check claim status
Deadline: Claims Being Processed

Many claims have already been distributed, but if you were notified and haven't filed, check the claims status immediately.

2. Visa and Mastercard Swipe Fee Settlement

$17.5 Billion

This is one of the largest antitrust class action settlements ever. Visa and Mastercard agreed to reduce swipe fees and provide rebates to merchants who were overcharged for processing credit card transactions. This settlement affects millions of small businesses, retailers, and restaurants that were charged excess fees for years.

Who Qualifies

  • Businesses that accepted Visa or Mastercard payments between 2004 and 2015
  • Retailers of any size (including online sellers on Amazon, eBay, Shopify)
  • Restaurants, gas stations, convenience stores, and service businesses
  • Small business owners and sole proprietors
  • Estates or successors of deceased business owners

How Much Can You Expect

  • Small businesses may receive $500 to $10,000+
  • Medium retailers often receive $5,000 to $50,000
  • Large merchants may receive significantly more based on processing volume
  • Payments are calculated based on transaction history during the class period
Est. Payout: $1,000-$25,000+ per business
Status: Claims Being Reviewed
Deadline: Extended

This settlement has an extended filing period. If you ran a business during 2004-2015, file immediately as payouts can be substantial.

3. Toyota Rust and Corrosion Settlement

$299 Million

Toyota agreed to settle claims that certain vehicles were prone to premature rust and corrosion, particularly on the undercarriage and suspension components. Affected owners reported extensive rust problems occurring well before typical vehicle lifespan, leading to safety concerns and significant repair costs.

Which Models Are Covered

  • 2004-2016 Toyota Sienna minivans
  • 2004-2013 Toyota Corolla sedans
  • 2004-2015 Toyota Camry sedans
  • 2005-2015 Toyota Tacoma pickup trucks
  • 2005-2015 Toyota Tundra pickup trucks
  • 2006-2011 Toyota RAV4 crossovers

What to Check

  • Inspect undercarriage, suspension, and frame for rust spots
  • Check underside of doors and panels for corrosion
  • Document rust damage with photos before filing
  • Keep repair receipts if you've already paid for rust-related fixes
Who Qualifies: Owners of affected models
Est. Payout: $200-$3,500 per vehicle
Deadline: Claims Still Open

The settlement covers both repair costs and diminished vehicle value. If you own an affected model, file even if you haven't had major rust problems, as coverage includes preventative treatment.

4. State Farm Auto Insurance Overcharge Settlement

$110 Million

State Farm agreed to settle claims that it improperly calculated customer insurance rates and overcharged policyholders. The company allegedly failed to apply available discounts and used flawed algorithms to set premiums for millions of customers.

Who Was Overcharged

  • State Farm auto insurance customers between 2015 and 2022
  • Customers whose rates increased without corresponding risk changes
  • Policyholders who didn't receive advertised discounts
  • Drivers whose premiums exceeded State Farm's published rates for similar coverage

How the Payout Works

  • Each eligible customer receives a pro-rata share of the settlement fund
  • Refunds are calculated based on how much you overpaid relative to what you should have paid
  • Checks are mailed automatically if you had active policies during the class period
  • No filing required for most customers (State Farm has your information)
Who Qualifies: Former State Farm customers
Est. Payout: $150-$600 per customer
Deadline: Claims Being Distributed

If you had State Farm coverage, check for settlement checks in your mail or contact the claims administrator to verify your eligibility.

5. Tinder and Match Group Dating App Settlement

$60.5 Million

Match Group (parent company of Tinder, Hinge, and Match.com) agreed to settle claims that it used dark patterns and deceptive practices to trick users into subscribing. The company allegedly made it extremely difficult to cancel subscriptions and continued charging users after they attempted to unsubscribe.

Who Qualifies

  • Users who subscribed to Tinder, Match, OkCupid, Hinge, or POF between 2016 and 2024
  • Anyone charged after attempting to cancel
  • Users who had difficulty finding or using the cancellation feature
  • Customers in states with specific consumer protection laws (California, New York, Illinois)

What Changed

  • Match Group must now provide clear cancellation options
  • Refunds issued to users who were improperly charged
  • Retroactive compensation for all affected subscribers
  • New disclosures about subscription terms at signup
Who Qualifies: Past subscribers (2016-2024)
Est. Payout: $50-$300 per user
Deadline: Filing Closes Soon

If you were a paid subscriber and felt the cancellation process was confusing or if you were charged after trying to cancel, file your claim immediately.

6. Major Healthcare Data Breach Settlement

$350 Million

One of the largest healthcare data breaches in history exposed the personal and medical information of over 30 million patients. The breach affected multiple health insurance providers and hospital networks, exposing SSNs, medical records, insurance details, and financial information.

Coverage Includes

  • Identity theft monitoring and restoration services (up to 6 years)
  • Out-of-pocket medical expenses related to breach (up to $5,000)
  • Cash compensation for time spent dealing with the breach
  • Reimbursement for fraud losses
Who Qualifies: Patients notified of the breach
Est. Payout: $50-$500+ per person
Deadline: Claims Still Open

7. Smartphone Throttling Settlement

$175 Million

A major smartphone manufacturer agreed to settle claims that it intentionally slowed older phone models through software updates without disclosing this to consumers. The company allegedly degraded performance to encourage users to purchase newer models.

Affected Models

  • Flagship models released between 2015 and 2019
  • Devices that received the "performance management" software update
  • Users who experienced significant slowdown after major iOS/Android updates
Who Qualifies: Owners of affected models (2015-2022)
Est. Payout: $35-$85 per device
Deadline: Filing Closes Soon

8. Banking Overdraft Fee Settlement

$120 Million

A large national bank settled claims that it reordered customer transactions to maximize overdraft fees. The bank allegedly processed the largest transactions first to drain accounts faster, deliberately triggering multiple smaller overdraft charges that wouldn't have occurred with normal chronological processing.

Impact of the Practice

  • Customers faced unexpected overdraft fees of $30 to $40+ per transaction
  • Multiple fees triggered on the same day from reordering transactions
  • Accounts were drained faster, making it harder to recover
  • Particularly harmed customers living paycheck to paycheck
Who Qualifies: Customers with overdraft fees (2019-2025)
Est. Payout: $50-$200 per customer
Deadline: Claims Still Open

9. Auto Defect Recall Settlement

$95 Million

An automaker settled claims related to a defective transmission system that caused unexpected acceleration, difficulty stopping, and loss of power. The settlement covers repair costs, diminished vehicle value, and direct cash payments for affected owners.

What You Get

  • Full transmission repair costs covered by settlement
  • Additional cash payment for inconvenience and time
  • Reimbursement if you already paid for repairs
  • Compensation for vehicle value loss
Who Qualifies: Owners of specified models (2020-2023)
Est. Payout: $200-$3,000+ depending on repairs
Deadline: Claims Still Open

Why Most People Leave Money on the Table

Settlement payouts total billions of dollars, yet millions of eligible people never file. Here's why.

1. They Don't Know About the Settlement

Claim notices are sent by mail or email, but many go unread or end up in spam folders. By the time people realize there's money to claim, the deadline has often passed. ClaimCheck helps you discover settlements you're eligible for before filing deadlines close.

2. The Process Seems Complicated

Settlement claims look intimidating with legal documents and complex instructions. In reality, most claims take 5 to 10 minutes to file online. No lawyers needed, no court appearances required.

3. They Underestimate the Payout

People see estimates like "$50-$200" and think it's not worth their time. What they don't realize: when few people file, payouts increase dramatically. With millions left unclaimed, the average payout can jump 3x to 5x higher than initial estimates.

4. They Miss Deadlines

Settlement deadlines are firm. Once they close, you lose any right to claim. Many people think they have more time than they actually do. Checking active settlements on ClaimCheck with deadline alerts ensures you never miss a filing window.

5. They Don't File Multiple Claims

Most people qualify for 2 to 5 settlements simultaneously (data breach, credit card fees, product defect, etc.). Those who file just one miss out on the others. Using ClaimCheck's settlement categories helps you identify all the claims you qualify for.

Critical Tip: Settlement claim windows are typically 1 to 3 years. If you received a claim notice, don't delay. File within 30 days of receiving notice to avoid last-minute deadlines. Once the deadline passes, you lose access to the settlement permanently.

How to Check If You Qualify for Multiple Settlements

Most people qualify for multiple settlements without realizing it. Here's how to identify them all.

Step 1: Start with Data Breaches

If you've received breach notification letters, you have active claims. Check the sender and search for the settlement on ClaimCheck by company name.

Step 2: Check Your Purchase History

Did you own a Toyota, Hyundai, or other recalled vehicle during the affected years? Keep VIN and ownership records handy. Defect settlements often have longer filing windows.

Step 3: Review Financial Services

Bank customers, credit card users, and insurance policyholders are frequent settlement targets. Review statements from the past 10 years for unusual fees or charges.

Step 4: Remember Digital Subscriptions

Paid apps, dating services, streaming platforms, and social media accounts you used between 2015 and 2024 may be covered by privacy or billing settlements.

Step 5: Search ClaimCheck

Use ClaimCheck's settlement database to filter by category, amount, or deadline to find all open claims matching your situation.

Pro Tip: Create a spreadsheet of all settlements you're eligible for, including deadlines. Set phone reminders for 1 week before each deadline. Filing takes 5-10 minutes, but missing a deadline costs you thousands.

The Settlement Filing Timeline

Understanding the settlement lifecycle helps you avoid missing deadlines.

Settlement Timeline

Event 1: Settlement Approved
Court approves settlement agreement. Companies begin mailing notices to affected customers.
Event 2: Claim Period Opens (Days 1-30)
This is your window to file. Some settlements give 1 year, others only 60 days. Act immediately upon receiving notice.
Event 3: Deadline Approaches
Final filing window closes. No claims accepted after this date, regardless of circumstance.
Event 4: Claims Administrator Reviews (Months 2-6)
Your claim is processed and verified. You may be asked for additional documentation.
Event 5: Payment Processing (Months 6-12)
Approved claims receive payment via check or direct deposit. Timing varies by settlement.
